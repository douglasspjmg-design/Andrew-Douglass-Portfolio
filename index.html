My Story

How I Actually Became a Project Manager

I didn't take the traditional path into project management. I started in the warehouse, shipping and retail, learning the business from the ground up. Around the time I found out I was going to be a father, I pitched the owner on a franchise expansion model to open a location in Pensacola. He saw a bigger opportunity for me instead — the company was about to go heavy into electric bikes, and he pointed me toward leading that growth.

I had no formal background in it, so I taught myself — research, videos, hands-on trial and error — while still carrying my normal day-to-day warehouse and retail responsibilities. I was the only one willing to take that on, so I made it mine. That's where I really became a project manager, not from a classroom, but from figuring it out in real time and owning the results.

Along the way, I managed a team of 5-10 employees, handling scheduling, accountability, and performance. No bachelor's degree, just seven years of hands-on work solving real problems, from keeping supply chains running through COVID to resolving quality issues that could have cost us a major account. My PMP wasn't something I chased for a resume line, it's a reflection of work I'd already been doing.

Since 2024, I've kept steady income through insurance sales and delivery driving while completing my PMP and finding the right next opportunity — that's the same ownership mindset, just applied to a career transition instead of a warehouse floor. That's the kind of project manager I am — I don't wait to be handed expertise, I go get it, and I bring that same ownership and honesty to every team I'm on.

Case Studies

Real Problems, Real Results

International Supply Chain Coordination During COVID-19

July 2020 – February 2022
ProblemSupply risk emerged from extended lead times and surging demand for outdoor recreation products, especially hydraulic brake components.
ApproachI evaluated alternative suppliers, coordinated directly with international manufacturers, managed prototype testing and validation, and directed air freight logistics for prototype shipments. I tracked supplier lead times and adjusted procurement strategy in real time.
ResultApproved components for production, coordinated container shipments (~$150K per order), and maintained product availability that supported revenue growth during peak demand.

Handlebar Quality Resolution for a Key Customer Account

2022
ProblemA high-volume customer account had recurring handlebar quality issues across a fleet of e-bikes deployed at 200-250 properties, risking the relationship.
ApproachI defined quality requirements, built a testing and validation strategy for replacement components, coordinated prototype testing (including brake component inspection under real-world conditions), and managed customer feedback loops. Resolved over roughly four months.
ResultSelected and implemented an approved handlebar solution, resolved the quality issue, and preserved the customer partnership across all deployed properties.

Digital Repair Workflow Implementation (Shopify)

Process Design & Implementation
ProblemRepair requests were handled entirely manually, causing communication delays, inconsistent customer experience, high administrative workload, and no visibility into repair status.
ApproachI identified the inefficiencies in the manual process and designed an end-to-end digital workflow using Shopify, covering the full customer journey: online repair request submission, digital invoicing, service scheduling, and streamlined payment. I built the customer-facing intake and service flow myself, led the implementation, and iterated on it based on real-world usage and feedback.
ResultSignificantly reduced manual administrative workload, improved customer transparency and experience, and created a scalable, repeatable workflow that increased efficiency across service operations.
